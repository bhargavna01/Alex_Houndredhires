## Content

A $50 billion company (IKEA) built an AI chatbot in 2021. Then —they did the opposite of firing all the people the chatbot “replaced.”

They named the bot Billie.

Billie was good. 
She answered half the questions. 

The other half, she could not.

Every other company at the table knew what to do next. 

You cut the people the machine replaced. 
You tell the board. 
You move on.

But…

IKEA did not do that.
     They kept all 8,500 of them.

Then — they did something stranger.

They looked at the questions Billie could not answer. 
They read them. 
They read them again.

The questions were not about delivery times. 
They were not about returns.

The questions were about homes.

Will this sofa fit. What goes with the kitchen. I do not know where to start.

The people were not calling for service.
They were calling for help.

So IKEA did something brilliant.

They taught the 8,500 humans on staff to be designers. Not customer service agents with a new title. Real designers.

They learned rooms. They learned light. They learned how to sit with a stranger over video and help her see her house the way she wanted to see it.

…

The first year, that work brought in $1.4 billion.

None of it existed before.

…

I think about this story often. I will tell you why.

We are living through a strange season. The headlines are loud. The fear is louder. Every week another company says the machine took the work, so the people had to go.

But the machine did not take the work.
The machine took the easy part of the work. The part that was already breaking the people doing it.

What is left is the part that matters.

The part that needs a human in the room. 

The part nobody was getting to before because the mundane, soulless part ate the whole day.

This is the future. It is not the one we were warned about.

The machine is not the enemy. 
The fear is.
The leaders who are afraid will cut, and they will save a little, and they will lose the billion that was sitting in the questions the machine could not answer.

The leaders who are not afraid will look at what the machine cannot do, and they will build there.

You get to choose which one you are.

…

I hope you choose well.

The future is not coming to take something from you.

It is coming to give you back the work you were meant to do.

Walk toward it.

What is the work the machine is giving back to you?
