## Content

Why your Claude Code Skills sound exactly like ChatGPT 3.5.

(And the "copy-paste" mistake ruining your agent's output).

I see people installing 100+ skills into Claude Code, hoping it magically builds a business. Then they wonder why the output is generic, forgettable, and breaks half the time.

More skills don't make Claude smarter. They make it confused.

Here are the 3 traps you're probably falling into (and how to fix them in 15 minutes):

1. The Hoarding Trap
You have 50 skills installed. Claude only has a 15k character budget to read them. It's a noisy menu. 
Fix: Delete until you have 20 curated, specific skills. If you can't explain why you need it, trash it.

2. The Copy-Paste Trap
You download a skill from GitHub and expect it to know your brand. It's like hiring a consultant and refusing to brief them.
Fix: Add reference files (brand voice, examples). Tell Claude to read them first.

3. The Cramming Trap
You put your process, voice, and rules into one massive skill.md file. This eats up tokens and makes debugging impossible.
Fix: Keep skill.md for the step-by-step process ONLY. Put context in separate reference files.

P.S. - Good systems take a few days to build. But when they run successfully at 3 AM without complaining, you'll understand why slow and steady beats fast and broken.
